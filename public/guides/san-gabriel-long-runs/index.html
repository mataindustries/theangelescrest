San Gabriel Long Runs That Build AC100 Fitness

Short answer: Build around repeatable long-run loops that stack climbing, technical descent, and heat exposure instead of chasing random mileage.

Last updated: 2026-02-18

What should be on your checklist?

Use this checklist to cover the essentials before race day or your next key training run.

  • Pick routes with sustained climbs and runnable descents.
  • Use one nutrition protocol every long run until it is automatic.
  • Add back-to-back long-run weekends every 3-4 weeks.
  • Log weather, pace drift, and leg durability after each run.

What steps should you follow?

Follow these steps in order so your plan stays simple and repeatable under AC100-style conditions.

  1. Start with a durable weekly baseline you can repeat for a month.
  2. Increase vert and time-on-feet in small, planned blocks.
  3. Layer in heat and night stressors after consistency is stable.
  4. Taper volume before key race rehearsals or qualifier events.
