Heat Safety for SoCal Trail Runners

Short answer: Slow down early, carry extra fluids and sodium, and stop immediately if confusion, chills, or nausea keep getting worse.

Last updated: 2026-02-18

What should be on your checklist?

Use this checklist to cover the essentials before race day or your next key training run.

  • Check canyon temperatures, not just your city forecast.
  • Carry enough fluids for the longest exposed segment.
  • Use a cooling plan: shade stops, ice, and wetting your head/neck.
  • Know your red-flag symptoms and exit points before you start.

What steps should you follow?

Follow these steps in order so your plan stays simple and repeatable under AC100-style conditions.

  1. Shift your start time earlier and reduce target pace for hot days.
  2. Hydrate and fuel on a schedule before you feel behind.
  3. At first warning signs, cool down and reassess within 5-10 minutes.
  4. If symptoms escalate, end the run and recover in shade immediately.
