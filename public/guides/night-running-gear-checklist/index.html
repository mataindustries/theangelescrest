Night Running Gear Checklist (Trail)

Short answer: Carry one reliable primary light, one true backup light, verified battery life, and enough layers to stay warm if you slow down.

Last updated: 2026-02-18

Checklist

  • Primary headlamp tested for full planned runtime.
  • Backup light accessible without removing your pack.
  • Spare battery or power bank plus charging cable.
  • Reflective layer, whistle, and emergency calories.

Steps

  1. Charge all devices the same day you run.
  2. Do a five-minute function check at the trailhead.
  3. Switch to backup before total battery failure if output drops.
  4. Turn around early if visibility, weather, or footing deteriorates.

For lighting specifics, see AC100 headlamps + battery plan.
