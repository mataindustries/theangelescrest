Sean O’Brien 50 Miler (Calabasas): AC100 Qualifier Guide

A strong “systems test” 50 for LA runners. The terrain rewards runners who can keep effort even, manage small climbs, and fuel without drama.

Calabasas / Santa Monica Mountains SoCal qualifier Pacing discipline

Why this race is a smart AC100 qualifier

  • Close to Los Angeles, so you can stay calm and arrive rested.
  • Rolling climbs force you to control effort repeatedly.
  • Great environment to practice a simple, repeatable fueling plan.

Course feel

This is not a “one big climb” mountain ultra. It’s repeated work. The difficulty is how small decisions add up: micro-surges, skipped calories, sloppy descents.

What to practice here for AC100

Pacing

  • Keep early climbs easy.
  • Run the flats at a sustainable cadence.
  • Don’t chase people who are burning matches.

Fueling

  • Set a timer for calories.
  • Alternate sweet/salty to avoid gut shutdown.
  • Drink to conditions; don’t under-salt if it’s warm.

LA logistics

  • From SGV, plan for freeway time. Arriving stressed is free fatigue.
  • Bring layers for early chill + later warmth. SoCal can swing.

Common mistakes

  • Racing the first half like a marathon.
  • Skipping calories because it “feels easy.”
  • Letting small descents trash your quads.
